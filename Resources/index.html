Propane Power Kits

Harnessing the Power of American Ingenuity

What if I were to tell you that there was a power source that is 80% cleaner than gasoline? What if I were to further tell you that this incredible power source was also cheaper per gallon? What if I were to stretch your imagination beyond what you thought was possible and I said that this power source was able to be mostly produced here in America? I imagine that you would think that it’s simply not possible. That would be an incredible world wouldn’t it? Well, this isn’t some exotic new particle we’re talking about, or even some sort of futuristic science fiction. This fuel is here and available today. It’s called propane.

You mean like I use in my grill? Yes, the very same propane that you use to power your grill can also be used to power your weed whacker, lawn mower, golf cart, rototiller, and just about anything else with a small engine. Sure, you’re probably thinking to yourself, but I bet it costs a fortune to buy devices like that. Yes, it does. That’s why a bunch of guys got together and figured out how to convert your gas guzzling, air polluting, old piece of machinery and make it more powerful, completely green, and cheaper to run.

As if that weren’t enough to pique your interest, this conversion can be done in as little as a half hour on average and doesn’t cost more than a typical repair to the same equipment would.

Alright, so what’s the downside? That’s the beauty of all of this. There is no down side. You will be paying 30 – 40% less money for the same functionality, and you get all the fun of being morally superior by using greener lawn equipment.

Here is a non-exclusive list of some of the things we’ve seen converted:

  • Back Pack Blowers
  • Commercial Blowers
  • Ditch Witcher
  • Generators
  • Outboard Motors
  • Lawnmowers (both commercial and domestic
  • Snow Blowers
  • Water Pumps
  • Weed Trimmers
  • and much more...

If you are still teetering on the brink of indecision, how about the fact that the United States has the highest concentrations of propane in the world and more than 90% of the propane used is from here? The manufacture of all AltFuel propane power kits is done here in the United States as well.
